1The 30-Second Pitch (memorise this)

"T.H.Y.N.K is a once-a-week, 35-minute program for Grades 4 to 7 that teaches Vedic Maths and Coding together — using your existing computer lab and your existing computer teacher. Children learn to think faster mentally, code their own games and websites, and even use AI as a thinking partner. We provide everything — teacher guides, student workbooks, starter files, training. The school provides the room, the teacher and the slot. ₹150 per session per child for a full year, with 20–25% revenue share back to the school."

2The Core Idea (one line)

  • A Vedic sutra is a step-by-step rule to reach an answer.
  • An algorithm is a step-by-step rule to reach an answer.
  • Same thinking. Two languages. We make the connection visible.

Always say this once in every meeting — it lands.

3Why Parents Should Enrol Their Child

a. Future-Readiness

  • By 2030, every job will have a digital component — medicine, agriculture, design, even sports.
  • A child who can't read code in 2030 = a child who couldn't read English in 2000.
  • AI is not replacing programmers — it is replacing people who can't work with programmers.
  • Children who code can direct AI; children who don't will be directed by it.

b. Sharper, Faster Brain

  • Vedic Maths children calculate 3–5× faster mentally than peers using traditional methods.
  • That speed transfers into school maths, science, and competitive exams.
  • Coding teaches structured thinking — break a big problem into small, testable steps.
  • Research (MIT, Harvard, Stanford): kids who learn Scratch show measurable gains in maths, reading and logical reasoning within 6–12 months.

c. Healthy Screen Time

  • Today's children spend hours consuming YouTube, games, reels.
  • T.H.Y.N.K flips that: in Week 1 the child becomes the maker of the game, not the player.
  • Same screen, completely different relationship — productive, creative, intentional.

d. Resilience & Confidence

  • Code rarely works the first time. Children learn to fail calmly, debug patiently, try again — the most important life skill a school can give.
  • Every project ends with the child saying "I made this!" — genuine confidence, not certificates.

e. Strong Indian Roots

  • Vedic Maths is India's own mathematical heritage — older than most Western methods.
  • Children grow up proud of their roots while being globally competitive.
  • Parents love that we're not blindly copying the West — we're connecting our wisdom to modern tools.

f. Affordable, Inside School

  • ₹150 per session for a full year — less than ONE private tuition class.
  • No need to send the child anywhere extra — it happens inside their existing school day / Saturday.
  • No commute, no extra time pressure, no extra tuition fatigue.

4What the Student Walks Away With

Use this when a parent asks: "But what will my child actually learn?"

Grades 4 & 5 — Scratch + Vedic Maths

  • Build their first 5 working games: Guess-My-Number, Nikhilam Subtraction Wizard, Times-Table Race, Urdhva Multiplier, and a Final Project of their own design.
  • Master sequencing, loops, variables, conditionals, operators — the foundation of every programming language.
  • Solve mental maths in seconds using sutras like Ekadhikena Purvena, Nikhilam, Urdhva-Tiryagbhyām.

Grades 6 & 7 — Real Web Programming + Vedic Maths

  • Learn the same three languages that built Google, Instagram and every website on the internet — HTML, CSS, JavaScript.
  • Build a real, published website by Week 24 — that the parent can visit on their phone.
  • Right-click "Inspect" on any website and understand what they're seeing.

And for both age groups —

  • Learn to talk to AI properly — frame prompts, refine answers, and use AI as a thinking partner (we teach this safely; teacher guide has a full chapter on age-appropriate AI prompting).
  • Speak with confidence — every session ends with the child explaining their work to the class.

5Why the School Should Include T.H.Y.N.K

This is the page that gets the principal to say yes. Memorise the 7 points.

a. NEP 2020 Compliance — Audit Ready

  • NEP 2020 explicitly mandates coding, computational thinking and digital fluency from middle school.
  • T.H.Y.N.K delivers this in a structured, measurable, audit-ready format — lesson plans, rubrics, certificates, progress trackers.
  • We hand the school a paper trail it can show to inspectors any day.

b. Zero Capital Cost — Plug & Play

  • Uses the school's existing computer lab, existing computer teacher, existing Saturday slot.
  • No new hardware. No software licences. No specialist hiring. No training fees for the school.
  • All materials are browser-based — works on any Windows, Mac or Chromebook.

c. Builds on What Schools Already Do

  • Most schools already touch Vedic Maths in middle school — we deepen and modernise it instead of replacing it.
  • We don't compete with the existing maths or computer curriculum — we strengthen both.

d. Teacher Empowerment, Not Replacement

  • We deliver a One-Day Teacher Training before the program starts.
  • The school's own computer teacher delivers the program — they grow professionally, the school retains the IP of skill.
  • We provide ongoing support, but the relationship with students stays with the school's teacher.

e. A Powerful Differentiator at Admissions

  • Parents today actively choose schools that prepare children for the future.
  • "Coding + Vedic Maths" is a memorable, premium-sounding program at admissions opens / parent meets.
  • Easy to feature on website, brochure, and tour script.

f. Genuine Academic Lift

  • Schools that piloted similar coding-with-maths programs report measurable improvement in maths scores within one academic year.
  • Soft skills (focus, teamwork, presentation) improve as well — visible in any classroom within 8 weeks.

g. Revenue Share — A Partnership, Not a Vendor Deal

  • We share 20–25% of the annual program fee back to the school (negotiable on cohort size).
  • The school earns from a flagship program at zero capital outlay.
  • Funds we share back can support the lab, the teacher, or the school's own future-skills initiatives.

7Anticipated Objections — How We Respond

Both of us must give the same answer. Memorise these.

We already do Vedic Maths in our school.
"Wonderful — that means your children are already half-way there. T.H.Y.N.K is not a Vedic Maths replacement; it's the coding application of what they already know. We sit on top of your existing programme and amplify it."
Our computer teacher doesn't know Vedic Maths.
"Perfectly fine. Our Teacher Guide assumes zero Vedic Maths background. The one-day training covers everything they need. The teacher learns alongside the students for the first 4 sessions — and then they're confident for life."
We already teach coding in school.
"Most schools teach Office, basic typing, or theory of computers. T.H.Y.N.K teaches building things — actual games, actual websites, actual AI use. There's no overlap; if anything, your existing IT class becomes the prep, and T.H.Y.N.K becomes the showcase."
Why not just use free YouTube tutorials?
"Because YouTube has no structure, no assessment, no teacher support, and no Vedic connection. A child clicking random videos learns scattered tricks; a child following T.H.Y.N.K builds a portfolio of 5 working projects in a year, with a teacher to debug with them."
What about screen time concerns?
"We agree completely — and that's exactly why we're here. The program is 35 minutes a week, supervised, building something meaningful. We're replacing 35 minutes of consumption with 35 minutes of creation."
₹3,600 per child — why this price?
"It's deliberately under ₹150 a session. Less than one private tuition class. We've kept it affordable so no parent has to choose between this and another activity. And 20–25% comes back to the school — so it's not a cost, it's a partnership."
What if the program flops mid-year?
"We have a midpoint review at Week 12 — you give us honest feedback, we adjust. We also offer a pilot cohort at no cost. If our promises don't show in 12 weeks, we walk away. We're confident enough to put that in writing."

9Words / Phrases — Use & Avoid

USE these — they land well

  • "Future-ready" / "Future-proof"
  • "From consumer to creator"
  • "Indian heritage, modern code"
  • "Plug-and-play for the school"
  • "Audit-ready, NEP-aligned"
  • "Partnership, not a vendor deal"
  • "Their first portfolio of real projects"
  • "Thinking partner, not answer machine" (about AI)
  • "By Week 24, the child will…"

AVOID these — they raise red flags

  • "Easy" / "Simple" — sounds shallow. Use "structured" or "step-by-step" instead.
  • "Everyone is doing this" — they're not, and the principal knows it.
  • "Coaching class" / "Tuition" — we're a program, not a tuition.
  • "Cheap" — we're affordable.
  • "Replace your teacher" — we empower the teacher.
  • Over-promising specific exam scores — promise skill, never rank.
